io.js Week of February 6th 2015

Atom & nw.js adopt io.js, Linux Tracing, and much more.

Every Friday we’re going to start posting weekly updates of the activity in and around io.js.

We looked back at all the contributions in January and found that it was the 3rd most active month in Node history (294 commits) and the single most active month in almost three years (308 commits in February 2012). With the contribution growth we’ve seen so far we expect February to be even more active.

  1. 1.1.0 was released, check out the** Changelog.**
  2. Simplified Stream Construction by Sam Newman lands.
  3. Linux Tracing (LTTNG) is being added.
  4. Google shares some of their tracing work** in v8 and Chrome** as we tighten up collaboration around io.js debugging efforts.
  5. io.js support added to Travis CI.
  6. Codeship supports io.js.
  7. Atom Editor moves to io.js.
  8. nw.js (formerly node-webkit) moves to io.js.
  9. New Tessel Hardware will support io.js.
  10. Chris Dickinson on-boarded six new committers, bringing the total number of active committers to 23.
  11. Working on a Stability and Compatibility Policy, participation from the community is encouraged.
  12. We’ve started reaching out to companies for feedback on our roadmap. If you have contact information for companies we may have missed let us know.
  13. Next week is Node Summit w/ many io.js people speaking and in attendance.
  14. Weekly TC Meeting.
  15. Website Working Group Meeting.
  16. Tracing Working Group Meeting.
